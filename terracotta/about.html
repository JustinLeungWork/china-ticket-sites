About us

We exist because the official system doesn't work for you

The Terracotta Warriors are one of the world's great archaeological sites. Getting a ticket to see them shouldn't require a Chinese phone number, WeChat Pay, and an Alipay account.

The problem we kept seeing

I grew up in Hong Kong and moved to Singapore a few years ago. Between both cities, I have a lot of friends from Korea, Japan, Indonesia, Thailand, and further afield who travel through China. A number of them had Xi'an on their itinerary and wanted to see the Terracotta Warriors — one of the few things in China that genuinely lives up to the hype.

The problem was always the same. The official booking site (bmy.com.cn) requires SMS verification through a Chinese phone number — not just any number, but a SIM registered on a Chinese carrier. Then it requires payment through Alipay or WeChat Pay, both of which need a Chinese bank account to fund properly. International cards are not accepted.

For a Chinese citizen living in mainland China, this is frictionless. For a Korean tourist arriving in Xi'an, or a French couple on a two-week China tour, it is a brick wall. My friends would either give up, pay a scalper, or pay two to three times the face price through an OTA that added its own fees on top.

The ticket itself costs ¥120 — about $17 USD. The problem was never cost. It was access.

What we do

Terracotta Tickets holds the necessary Chinese phone numbers and payment accounts to book through bmy.com.cn directly at the official price. You submit your visit date, time slot, and passport details. We submit the booking on your behalf, receive the confirmation, and email you the QR codes — one per person — within 24 hours.

You pay us in USD with any standard card through Stripe. We handle the Chinese side. Your QR codes are the same official museum tickets that any Chinese visitor gets — same format, same entry process, same gate.

We also offer private English-speaking guides for groups who want more context than the museum's printed panels provide. That's entirely optional and booked separately after your ticket is confirmed.

What we are not

We are not affiliated with the Emperor Qinshihuang's Mausoleum Site Museum or any Chinese government body. We are a private concierge service. The museum ticket price is fixed by the museum at ¥120 — we charge a transparent service fee on top of that to cover the booking process and our time.

We are also not a tour operator. We do not arrange transport, hotels, or itineraries. We book tickets. If you want a guide, we can introduce one. Everything else is yours to arrange.
