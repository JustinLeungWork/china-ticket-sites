How to Visit Mutianyu Great Wall

Getting there from Beijing, cable car vs steps, best season to go, what to wear, and everything else you need to plan a great visit to the most scenic section of the Great Wall.

Quick answers

Where is Mutianyu Great Wall?

Mutianyu Great Wall (慕田峪长城) is in Huairou District, about 70–80 km northeast of central Beijing. It takes roughly 1.5–2 hours by car. The wall is set among forested mountains and is widely considered the most scenic section open to visitors — less crowded than Badaling, better preserved than most sections, and with a cable car, steps, and a toboggan slide all on the same stretch.

How do I get to Mutianyu from Beijing?

By car hire or taxi: the most comfortable option for most visitors. Budget 1.5–2 hours each way depending on traffic; ask your hotel to book a car or use the DiDi app. A day return typically costs ¥400–600 by private car. By tour bus: several operators run daily buses from Beijing hotels and train stations — check with your accommodation. By public transport: subway Line 5 to Dongzhimen, then Bus H25 or H28 to the wall — budget about 2.5 hours each way and check current bus schedules before you travel.

Should I take the cable car or walk up?

If your priority is walking along the wall itself and enjoying the views from up top, take the cable car (included in the Full Experience ticket). The steps are genuinely steep and the climb takes 20–40 minutes — fine if you're physically up for it, but tiring before you've even started exploring the wall. The toboggan slide is a fun way to descend and can be purchased separately on-site. Many visitors take the cable car up and the toboggan down.

What are the opening hours?

Mutianyu Great Wall is open daily 07:30–18:00, with last admission at 17:00. No fixed time slot is required — arrive any time during opening hours. Open year-round, including public holidays (though crowds are much higher during Chinese Golden Week in May and October).

About Mutianyu Great Wall

Mutianyu is one of the best-preserved sections of the Great Wall, originally built during the Northern Qi dynasty (550–577 CE) and substantially rebuilt during the Ming dynasty (1368–1644). Its 22 watchtowers, set along a 2.25 km open stretch of wall, offer spectacular views over forested mountains that change dramatically with the seasons — deep green in summer, brilliant gold and red in autumn, snow-covered in winter.

Unlike Badaling (the most visited section, about 80 km northwest of Beijing), Mutianyu sits in lush forest terrain and tends to attract fewer day-trippers. It's the section of choice for visitors who want a more atmospheric experience — and for those who want the cable car and toboggan combination.

Getting there from Beijing

  • By private car or taxi: The most practical option for most foreign visitors. Book through your hotel or use DiDi (China's ride-hail app — requires a Chinese phone number, but many hotels can help). Budget 1.5–2 hours each way depending on traffic. Leave by 08:00 to avoid the morning rush on the highway. A day-return trip typically costs ¥400–600 by private car.
  • By tour bus: Several operators run daily coach services from central Beijing (Dongzhimen area, Wangfujing, etc.) to Mutianyu. Book through your hotel or a reputable booking platform. Journey time is roughly 2 hours. A convenient, no-hassle option if you don't want to navigate independently.
  • By public transport: Take subway Line 5 (or Line 13) to Dongzhimen, then Bus H25 or H28 from Dongzhimen bus station to Mutianyu. Buses run from approximately 07:00; the journey takes about 1.5 hours. Check current timetables before travelling — services are less frequent in winter. Return buses stop running in the late afternoon so time your departure from the wall accordingly.
Tip: Leave central Beijing no later than 08:00 to arrive before the main coach groups, which typically reach the wall from about 10:00. The wall is noticeably quieter before 09:30.

Cable car, steps, or toboggan?

Mutianyu has three ways up and two ways down:

  • Steps (up and down): Around 1,000 steps to the wall. Steep, particularly the final section. Takes 20–40 minutes depending on fitness. Not recommended for young children or anyone with knee or mobility issues. You'll feel it the next day. That said, the climb itself offers views through the forest that the cable car misses.
  • Cable car gondola (up and/or down): The gondola lifts you directly to Tower 14 near the centre of the open stretch. Smooth, scenic, about 5 minutes. Highly recommended for families with children, older visitors, anyone prioritising time on the wall over the climb. Included in the Full Experience ticket we sell.
  • Toboggan (down only): A metal sliding track from Tower 6 to the base — roughly 1,580 metres of controlled descent. Very popular with children and adults. Not included in our tickets — purchased on-site. Operates seasonally; check availability at the attraction on the day.
Popular combination: cable car up, walk the wall, toboggan down. Efficient, fun, and saves your knees for the rest of the trip.

Best time to visit

Spring (April – mid-May): The wall is framed by flowering trees and fresh green leaves. Comfortable temperatures (15–25°C). One of the best times — but avoid the 1–7 May Golden Week holiday when crowds spike sharply.

Summer (June – August): Lush green forest backdrop, but hot and humid. Rain is possible (bring a waterproof layer). Weekdays are far quieter than weekends. The wall is beautiful in mist and cloud if you don't mind reduced views.

Autumn (September – October): The most spectacular season. The forested hills turn gold and red, and the wall offers some of its most photogenic views of the year. Go in late September or the first week of November to avoid the 1–7 October Golden Week rush.

Winter (November – March): Cold (often below 0°C at the wall), but dramatically beautiful when there's snow. Much quieter. Wear multiple layers and proper footwear — ice on the steps can be treacherous. The cable car may run limited hours; check ahead. Some facilities close.

Avoid: Chinese Golden Week (1–7 May and 1–7 October) and summer weekend afternoons. These are the peak crowds at any Great Wall section.

What to wear and bring

What to wear

  • Comfortable, closed-toe shoes — the steps and wall surface are uneven and can be slippery after rain
  • Layers — it's noticeably cooler on the wall than at the base in spring and autumn
  • Sunscreen and a hat in summer — exposed sections get direct sun
  • A waterproof layer in summer — afternoon storms are common in July and August
  • Warm coat and gloves in winter — the wall is significantly colder than Beijing city

What to bring

  • Original passport — required at the gate; name must match your ticket exactly
  • Water and snacks — the on-site café is limited; the views are better with your own food
  • Cash (RMB/yuan) — for the toboggan and any on-site extras; Chinese payment app is also accepted
  • Camera — photography is permitted everywhere; no drone flights allowed
  • Portable phone charger — useful for a long day out

On-site at Mutianyu

  • Shuttle bus: eco-bus from the main gate to the wall base — saves a 15-minute uphill walk
  • Cable car gondola: 5-minute ride to Tower 14 on the wall
  • Toboggan slide: 1,580m metal track for the descent — purchased on-site; seasonal operation
  • Restaurants and snack bars: at the base area; limited quality, limited hours in winter
  • Souvenir shops: at the base and near the cable car station
  • Toilets: at the base and at various points along the wall
  • Photography: permitted throughout; no drone flights allowed
