Booking guide

How to Buy Mutianyu Great Wall Tickets Without WeChat or Alipay

China's official Mutianyu ticketing site (mutianyu.com) requires Alipay or WeChat Pay — both tied to Chinese bank accounts. International visitors with foreign cards cannot complete the purchase. Here's how our service works instead:

  1. Choose your date. Unlike some attractions, Mutianyu has no fixed hourly entry slots — you arrive any time during opening hours (07:30–18:00). We need at least 2 days' notice to process your booking on the Chinese system.
  2. Submit passport details for every visitor. China's real-name system links each ticket to one passport. You provide name, passport number, and date of birth for each person in your group.
  3. Pay by card in USD. Visa, Mastercard, and Apple Pay accepted. Your passport data is stored securely and deleted automatically after your visit date.
  4. We submit to the Chinese system on your behalf. Within 24 hours we enter each visitor's details into China's official ticketing system and obtain the entry QR codes linked to your passports.
  5. Show your QR code and passport at the gate. Staff check both your QR code (on your phone) and your physical passport at the entrance — no printed ticket needed.

Mutianyu is about 70–80 km northeast of central Beijing — allow 1.5–2 hours travel time. Leave before 08:00 to beat peak crowds. The wall is busiest between 10:00 and 14:00.
