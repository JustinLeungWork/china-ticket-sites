About us

We exist because the official system doesn't work for you

Mutianyu Great Wall is one of the most spectacular things you can do near Beijing. Getting a ticket to go there shouldn't require a Chinese phone number, Chinese payment app, and an Alipay account.

The problem we kept seeing

I grew up in Hong Kong and moved to Singapore a few years ago. Between both cities, I have a lot of friends from Korea, Japan, Indonesia, Thailand, and further afield who travel through China. A number of them had Beijing on their itinerary and wanted to see the Great Wall — and Mutianyu is almost always the recommendation.

The problem was always the same. The official booking site (mutianyu.com) requires SMS verification through a Chinese phone number — not just any number, but a SIM registered on a Chinese carrier. Payment goes through Chinese payment apps, both of which require a Chinese bank account to fund properly for foreign visitors. International cards are not reliably accepted.

For a Chinese citizen living in mainland China, this is frictionless. For a French couple on a two-week China trip, or a Korean solo traveller arriving in Beijing, it is a brick wall. My friends would either give up and just turn up hoping for walk-in tickets, pay a tour operator two or three times the price, or spend an hour in frustration on apps that weren't built for them.

The ticket itself — admission plus a cable car — costs well under ¥250. The problem was never cost. It was access.

What we do

Mutianyu Wall Tickets holds the necessary Chinese phone numbers and payment accounts to book through mutianyu.com directly at the official price. You submit your visit date, passport details, and your ticket choice. We submit the booking on your behalf, receive the confirmation, and email you the QR codes — one per person — within 24 hours.

You pay us in USD with any standard card through Stripe. We handle the Chinese side. Your QR codes are the same official tickets that any Chinese visitor gets — same format, same entry process, same gate.

We offer three ticket types matching the official tiers: Admission Only, Admission + Shuttle Bus, and the Full Experience (Admission + Shuttle + Round-trip Cable Car). No guide upsell, no add-ons to decide — just the ticket you need for your visit.

What we are not

We are not affiliated with Mutianyu Great Wall or any Chinese government body. We are a private concierge service. Ticket prices at the attraction are set by the attraction — we charge a transparent service fee on top to cover the booking process and our time.

We are also not a tour operator. We do not arrange transport, hotels, or itineraries. We book tickets. Everything else — getting there, what to do near the wall, where to eat — is your call. Our Visitor Guide covers the practical side of the trip.
