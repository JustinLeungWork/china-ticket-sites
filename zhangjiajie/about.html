About us

Why this service exists

China's official park ticketing system requires a Chinese phone number, WeChat Pay, and Alipay — all unavailable to most international visitors. We handle the booking for you.

The problem we solve

Zhangjiajie National Forest Park (Wulingyuan Scenic Area) uses China's real-name reservation system. Booking a ticket requires SMS verification via a Chinese phone number, and payment only through Alipay or WeChat Pay — neither of which international visitors can easily access without a Chinese bank account.

International travellers wanting to visit the park either scramble for workarounds on arrival, rely on tour groups, or miss the park entirely. We built this service to close that gap: you pay in USD with any standard card, we handle the Chinese-side booking, and your QR codes arrive by email before your visit.

This is not a tour operator or travel agent. We are a ticket procurement service — we buy the same official Wulingyuan tickets that Chinese visitors buy, and pass them on to you at a transparent price. We are not affiliated with Wulingyuan Scenic Area or any Chinese government body.

Who we are

We are a small, Singapore-based team. Our founder, Justin Leung, grew up in Hong Kong and has spent years navigating the gap between international travellers and China's increasingly digitised attractions. After watching friends miss out on Zhangjiajie — or scramble to buy tickets on arrival with no guarantee of availability — the solution seemed obvious: someone with the right accounts and local knowledge should do this full-time.

We are not a big platform. We are a focused service that does one thing well: get international visitors into Zhangjiajie National Forest Park without friction.

How it works

  • You book on our site — select your first entry date, ticket type, and number of adult and youth visitors. Provide each visitor's passport details (required by China's real-name system).
  • You pay in USD via Visa, Mastercard, or Apple Pay through Stripe. No WeChat Pay, Alipay, or Chinese payment required.
  • We buy the official tickets through the Wulingyuan booking system using our authorised accounts — the same tickets any Chinese visitor would buy.
  • Your QR codes arrive by email within 24 hours of payment. One QR code per passport. Show it at the gate each day you enter, alongside your original passport.

Children under 14 enter free

Children under 14 enter Zhangjiajie National Forest Park for free — no ticket needed, from us or anyone else. Just bring their passport. We only sell tickets for adults and youth aged 14–17 (who receive a discounted rate and also need their passport for age verification at the gate).

Passport data and privacy

We take data seriously. China's real-name system legally requires your name and passport number — there is no way around this. We store your passport details only as long as needed to fulfil your booking, then delete them automatically 5 days after your first entry date (covering the full 4-day validity window). We never sell or share your data for marketing.

Full details in our Privacy Policy, which follows Singapore's Personal Data Protection Act 2012 (PDPA).
