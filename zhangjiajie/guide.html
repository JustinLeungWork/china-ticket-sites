How to Visit Zhangjiajie National Forest Park

Getting there, the Avatar filming spots, recommended itinerary, cable car tips, accommodation near the park, and what to bring — everything you need to plan your visit to Wulingyuan.

Quick answers

Where is Zhangjiajie National Forest Park?

Zhangjiajie National Forest Park is in Wulingyuan District, Zhangjiajie City, Hunan Province, China. The official park entrance is at Wulingyuan. Note that "Zhangjiajie city" and "Wulingyuan" are different places — they are about 35 km apart. Stay near Wulingyuan for easy park access.

How do I get to Zhangjiajie?

Fly to Zhangjiajie Hehua Airport (DYG) — about 30 minutes by taxi to Wulingyuan. Or take a high-speed train from Changsha South (长沙南) to Zhangjiajie Railway Station (~2.5 hours), then taxi to Wulingyuan (~30–45 minutes). There is no train to the park entrance itself.

Where was Avatar filmed?

The "Hallelujah Mountains" of Avatar were inspired by the sandstone pillars of Yuanjiajie. The specific peak officially renamed the "Avatar Hallelujah Mountain" is the South Sky Column (南天一柱) — visible from the main viewing platform in Yuanjiajie. It is one of the most photographed spots in the park.

How many days do you need?

Most visitors need 2–3 full days. The ticket is valid for 4 consecutive days — enough to cover all major areas without rushing. Day 1: Yuanjiajie + Tianzishan. Day 2: Huangshi Village + Golden Whip Stream. Day 3: Yangjiajie. Day 4: optional Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon or flex day.

About the park

Zhangjiajie National Forest Park (张家界国家森林公园) was China's first national forest park, established in 1982 and part of the UNESCO World Natural Heritage-listed Wulingyuan Scenic Area (designated 1992). The park is famous for its more than 3,000 quartzite sandstone pillars and peaks rising dramatically from the forest — some reaching over 200 metres. The unique landscape, draped in subtropical vegetation and often wreathed in mist, inspired the floating "Hallelujah Mountains" in James Cameron's Avatar (2009) and directly drove a wave of international recognition.

The park covers four main scenic areas: Yuanjiajie, Tianzishan, Yangjiajie, and Suoxiyu (which includes Golden Whip Stream and Huangshi Village). An extensive network of walkways, escalators, cable cars, and the famous Bailong Elevator connect the areas. The eco-friendly shuttle buses run continuously within the park and are included in the admission ticket.

Getting there

  • By air: Fly to Zhangjiajie Hehua Airport (DYG). Connections from Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Chengdu, and some international hubs. Taxi from airport to Wulingyuan ~30 minutes, ¥80–120.
  • By high-speed train: From Changsha South (长沙南) to Zhangjiajie Railway Station (张家界站) — about 2.5 hours on G-series trains. From the station, take a taxi to Wulingyuan (~35 km, 30–45 min, ¥70–100). There is no train to the park entrance.
  • From Changsha city: Changsha is the capital of Hunan and a common entry point for international visitors. The high-speed rail journey is the most comfortable option.
  • By taxi within the area: DiDi (China's ride-hailing app) works in Zhangjiajie city but coverage is limited in Wulingyuan. Negotiate with local taxis for day trips if DiDi is unavailable.
Wulingyuan vs Zhangjiajie city: The park entrance is at Wulingyuan (武陵源), not in Zhangjiajie city. They are 35 km apart. If you're staying for the park, book accommodation in Wulingyuan or within 5 km of the North Gate. Zhangjiajie city hotels are cheaper but require a 45-minute taxi each way.

Recommended 4-day itinerary

This covers all the major areas at a comfortable pace using your 4-day ticket. Adjust based on your fitness and interests.

Yuanjiajie (袁家界) + Tianzishan (天子山)

This is the Avatar zone — the South Sky Column (南天一柱) and the Hallelujah Mountain viewpoint are here. Take the Tianzishan cable car up in the morning when it's less crowded. Walk the Yuanjiajie loop, which takes 2–3 hours. In the afternoon, head to Tianzishan for the Emperor's Pen peak and the Xiannu Disseminating Flowers viewpoint.

The eco-bus connects both areas. Return via the Tianmen Mountain road if you have energy, or take the bus back to the park entrance.

Huangshi Village (黄石寨) + Golden Whip Stream (金鞭溪)

Huangshi Village is the largest peak accessible by cable car or a tough stair climb (3,800+ steps — the cable car is strongly recommended). The Five Fingers Peak and the Marshal Platform viewpoints are the highlights. Allow 3 hours on top. In the afternoon, walk Golden Whip Stream — a 7.5 km flat valley walk through the forest. Often foggy and atmospheric; monkeys visible if you're lucky.

Yangjiajie (杨家界) + Bailong Elevator (百龙天梯)

Yangjiajie is the quietest of the main areas and arguably the most dramatic — the Tianbo Mansion (天波府) platform looks across a sea of pillars. Take the Yangjiajie cable car up, walk the loop, and descend via the glass-floored walkways. Then ride the Bailong Elevator back up from the valley floor — 330 metres in 2 minutes in a glass-sided outdoor elevator built into a cliff face. Genuinely impressive engineering. Ride it once in each direction if you have the bundle.

Flex day — Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon or revisit

The Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon (张家界大峡谷) and its glass bridge (the longest and highest glass bridge in the world when built) is just outside the park and requires a separate ticket. Worth it if you have a full day spare. Alternatively, use day 4 to revisit a favourite area at sunrise (Golden Whip Stream at 7am is magical and almost empty), or explore the Tianmen Mountain Scenic Area (also outside the park, also separate ticket).

Timing tips

  • Queues are longest 9:00–11:00am — arrive early or wait until after lunch
  • 14:00–16:00 is the best time for shorter cable car queues
  • The Bailong Elevator queues grow from 10am — get there at opening or mid-afternoon
  • Mist is thickest in the morning — dramatic for photography, but some views are obscured
  • Rain can cause temporary trail closures — check before heading out

What to bring

Essentials

  • Original passport — required every day you enter the park
  • Comfortable walking shoes with grip (some paths are steep and slippery)
  • Rain jacket or compact umbrella — mist and rain are common year-round
  • Light layer / fleece even in summer — altitude means cooler temperatures at the top
  • Water and snacks — onsite restaurants are convenient but pricey
  • Portable phone charger — the park is large and navigation uses battery
  • Chinese app or Baidu Maps for navigation inside the park (Google Maps has limited coverage here)

Good to know

  • Photography is permitted everywhere — no tripod restrictions on most paths
  • The park is extensive — bring 15,000–20,000 steps per day of walking stamina
  • Mobile data: China SIM or international roaming required; Wi-Fi is not available in the park
  • Altitude: the highest viewing platforms are around 1,200m — nothing extreme but keep it in mind
  • Children under 14: free entry with passport — bring it for the gate
  • Youth aged 14–17: bring passport for age verification at the gate for discounted tickets

Best season to visit

  • Spring (April–May): Wildflowers, green forest, moderate crowds. The best balance of clear skies and comfortable temperatures. Highly recommended.
  • Summer (June–August): Hot and humid in the valleys, dramatic mist around the pillars. Spectacular photography conditions but heavy rain can close trails temporarily. Peak domestic tourist season.
  • Autumn (October–November): Golden foliage, clearer skies, cooler temperatures. Equal to spring for the visitor experience. Watch out for Golden Week (1–7 October) — extreme crowds.
  • Winter (December–March): Snow-dusted pillars are uniquely beautiful and crowd-free. Some trails and cable cars may close due to ice. The experience is quiet and striking if trails are open.
  • Avoid: Chinese national holidays — Golden Week (early October) and Spring Festival (late January to early February). The park becomes extremely crowded, queues are 2–3 hours, and accommodation prices surge.

Where to stay

For park access, stay in Wulingyuan town or within 5 km of the park's North Gate. There are dozens of hotels and guesthouses at every price point. The Wulingyuan core scenic area itself has a handful of hotels inside the park (pricier and quieter).

Zhangjiajie city has more hotel variety and better restaurants, but the 35 km commute eats into your day — only worthwhile if you plan to see Tianmen Mountain (which is near the city) rather than the forest park.

Book accommodation well in advance for Golden Week or any Chinese school holiday period — the area becomes fully booked weeks ahead.

The Avatar connection

James Cameron's production team visited Zhangjiajie in 2008 while scouting locations for Avatar. The towering sandstone pillars of the Yuanjiajie area inspired the floating Hallelujah Mountains on Pandora. After the film's release in 2009 the park officially renamed the "Southern Sky Column" peak (南天一柱) to the "Avatar Hallelujah Mountain" (哈利路亚山). A sign and marker at the Yuanjiajie viewpoint commemorates this connection.

The pillar that most closely resembles the film's imagery is best seen from the Yingbin Terrace viewpoint in Yuanjiajie — the one with the rickety wooden pavilion perched at its edge. Early morning mist makes it particularly cinematic.
