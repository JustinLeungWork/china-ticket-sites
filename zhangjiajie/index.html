About Zhangjiajie National Forest Park

Zhangjiajie became China's first national forest park in 1982, and its unearthly landscape of over 3,000 sandstone pillar formations rising from the subtropical forest floor has made it one of the country's most visited natural attractions. Director James Cameron cited the park as a direct inspiration for the floating Hallelujah Mountains in Avatar — a connection that drew international attention after the film's 2009 release. The park covers 4,810 hectares within the larger Wulingyuan Scenic Area, a UNESCO World Natural Heritage Site since 1992. Three main areas — Yuanjiajie, Tianzishan, and Suoxiyu — each have their own character and viewpoints.

Best time to visit

Spring (April–May) and autumn (September–November) offer the clearest skies and most dramatic cloud inversions — when the peaks disappear into mist and re-emerge like islands above the clouds. Summer (June–August) is lush and green but brings rain and reduced visibility. Winter visits are possible but fog can persist for days. The park is famously photogenic in light rain, when the mist hangs between the pillars. Avoid the October Golden Week and the May Labour Day holiday — the park becomes genuinely crowded. Aim to arrive at opening and head straight to Yuanjiajie via the Bailong Elevator before the tour groups arrive.

Getting to Zhangjiajie

  • By air: Zhangjiajie Hehua International Airport connects to Beijing (2h), Shanghai (2h), Chengdu (1.5h), and other major cities. Airport to park entrance is around 30 minutes by taxi.
  • By high-speed rail: Zhangjiajie West station on the Zhanghuai high-speed line. From Changsha (capital of Hunan) the journey takes around 1h 40m.
  • From the city: Taxis and DiDi (Chinese Uber) run to all three park entrances. Wulingyuan Gate is the most common entry point for the main Yuanjiajie area.

What to bring

  • Original passport — required at every gate scan, must match ticket name exactly
  • Sturdy walking shoes — some paths are unpaved and steep
  • Rain jacket — weather changes fast and light rain is common year-round
  • Cash or Alipay/WeChat Pay for food stalls and vendors inside the park
  • Power bank — the park is large and navigation apps drain battery quickly
  • Download offline maps before entering — mobile signal is patchy in the gorges
