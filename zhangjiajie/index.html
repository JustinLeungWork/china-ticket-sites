What to see

3,000+ sandstone pillars, the Avatar Hallelujah Mountains, cable cars above the clouds, and one of the world's most dramatic outdoor elevators.

🏔

Yuanjiajie — The Avatar Zone

The South Sky Column, officially renamed the "Avatar Hallelujah Mountain," stands here. The Yingbin Terrace viewpoint gives the iconic floating-mountains view. Best with morning mist.

🚡

Bailong Elevator

330 metres in 2 minutes, in a glass-sided outdoor elevator built into the cliff face. The world's highest and heaviest outdoor elevator. Included in the Full Transport Bundle.

🌿

Golden Whip Stream

A 7.5 km flat valley walk beneath the pillars, following a crystal-clear mountain stream. Monkeys often visible. Almost empty in the early morning. One of the park's most peaceful spots.

🏯

Tianzishan & Huangshi Village

The Emperor's Pen peak and the expansive Five Fingers viewpoint. Huangshi Village cable car climbs 1,000m in minutes. Views across thousands of pillars on a clear day.

Practical information

Getting there

  • ✈Fly to Zhangjiajie Hehua Airport (DYG) — 30 min taxi to Wulingyuan
  • 🚄High-speed train from Changsha South ~2.5 hours, then taxi to Wulingyuan
  • 🚕Stay near Wulingyuan, not Zhangjiajie city — they are 35 km apart

On the day

  • 📘Bring original passport every day — name must match exactly
  • 👟Comfortable shoes with grip — some paths are steep
  • 🌧Rain jacket — mist and showers are common year-round
  • 🧥Light jacket even in summer — altitude means cooler air at the top

Booking rules

  • 📅Book at least 2 days before your first entry date (Beijing time)
  • 📆Max date: 31 December 2026
  • 🎟One QR code per passport — groups get one code per person
  • 👶Children under 14 enter free — no booking needed from us

Best time to visit

  • 🌸Spring (April–May): wildflowers, clear skies, moderate crowds
  • 🍂Autumn (Oct–Nov): golden foliage, best visibility
  • ❄Winter: snow on the pillars — dramatic but some closures
  • ⚠Avoid Golden Week (early Oct) and Spring Festival — extreme crowds

Zhangjiajie National Forest Park uses China's real-name ticketing system, which legally requires every visitor's full name and passport number. One ticket is registered to one passport. The name on the ticket must match your passport exactly at the gate on each day you enter. We cannot work around this — it is a requirement of the Chinese park authority.
